J	Vojtyla, P				Vojtyla, P			Calibration of monitors used for surveillance of radioactivity in effluent water from CERN's accelerator installations	APPLIED RADIATION AND ISOTOPES			English	Article						efficiency calibration; computer simulation; monitoring of radioactivity; effluent water		Water released into the environment from CERN's accelerator installations may contain both long-lived (Be-7, Na-22) and short-lived (C-11, N-13, Na-24) gamma radioactivity. Each potential release point is equipped with an on-line monitor for short-lived radionuclides. which consists of a scintillation probe immersed in a tank filled with monitored water. Whilst calibration standards are available for long-lived radioactivity, computer simulations are the only feasible way to determine the monitor efficiency for the short-lived radionuclides. The paper describes computer simulations using the Monte Carlo code GEANT 3.21.
