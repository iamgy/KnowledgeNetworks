On comparisons of potential repository sites	ATW-INTERNATIONAL JOURNAL FOR NUCLEAR POWER			German	Article								This contribution deals with the question to what extent the selection of the best site for a repository for high-level radioactive waste by site comparison can be guided by safety-related criteria. The first difficulty encountered is due to the fact that no quantifiable basis can be assigned to the term "safety" Consequently, various indicators must serve as relative yardsticks. The indicators considered are pollutant retention, radiotoxicity, and individual dose as well as the durability and reliability of the insulation and the extent of uncertainties in safety evaluation. The article arrives at the conclusion that the indicators, to which limits can be assigned, determining the suitability or non-suitability of a site are only of limited use as yardsticks in quantifying the extent of safety of sites in a site comparison. On the whole, safety requirements are so high that different margins relative to the limits applying or to be applied to them would not represent a reasonable gain in safety. Especially quantifiable safety indicators are limited by the constraint that the observance of limits must be ensured not only by the properties of a site, and these indicators consequently stand more for the quality of the entire repository concept than for the quality of a site. The use of both quantitative and qualitative safety indicators as a yardstick in site selection is problematic also because an unambiguous result of comparison requires weighting of the indicators to have been fixed, which cannot be derived on scientific grounds.
