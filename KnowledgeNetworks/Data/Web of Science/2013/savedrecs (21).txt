DEVELOPMENT OF AN INHALATION INTAKE MODEL FOR Am-241 BASED ON MAYAK PRODUCTION ASSOCIATION WORKER DATA	HEALTH PHYSICS			English	Article						Am-241; dose, internal; modeling, dose assessment; nuclear workers	STATES TRANS-URANIUM; EXTRAPULMONARY ORGAN DISTRIBUTION; BIOKINETIC MODEL; PLUTONIUM; AMERICIUM; DOSIMETRY; EXCRETION; REGISTRY; RETENTION; SAMPLES	Americium-241 is a significant radiation hazard at facilities that handle or reprocess spent nuclear fuels. An inhalation intake model for Am-241 was developed using autopsy data obtained from former workers at the Radiochemical and Plutonium Production Plants at the Mayak Production Association (Mayak PA), Ozyorsk, Russia. Accumulation of Am-241 in the body can occur though direct exposure to Am-241 (termed here "exogenous" exposures), usually as an inhaled aerosol, or though exposure to Pu-241 that decays inside the body to Am-241 (termed here "endogenous" exposures). Metabolism of endogenous and exogenous Am-241 can differ, with endogenous Am-241 being initially related to the behavior of Pu-241. For the model, it was assumed that intakes of Am-241 and Pu-241 were functionally associated with intakes of Pu-239. The current Mayak Worker Dosimetry System model (MWDS-2008) was used to describe metabolism of plutonium and americium in the respiratory tract. The ICRP-30 model was used for the gastrointestinal tract, the ICRP-67 model was used for metabolism after absorption into the blood for americium, and the "Leggett modification" of the ICRP-67 model for plutonium was used for systemic, non-pulmonary organs. The proposed inhalation intake model for americium provides estimates for internal doses from Am-241 from both exogenous and endogenous sources.
